About Bhagavad Gita

The Bhagavad Gita, often referred to as the "Song of God," is a 700-verse Vedic scripture that is part of the Indian epic Mahabharata.

It is a sacred dialogue between Lord Krishna and the warrior prince Arjuna, taking place on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. This timeless scripture addresses the most profound questions of human existence - duty, righteousness, devotion, and the path to liberation.
